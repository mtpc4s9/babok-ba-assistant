### Financial Analysis (BABOK 10.20)

#### Overview
Financial analysis is the assessment of the expected financial viability of an investment option. It evaluates the total cost of the change, the total cost of ownership (TCO), and the expected benefits to determine whether a change initiative should be funded, adjusted, or stopped.

#### When to Use
*  User needs to justify a business case to the Board of Directors or Sponsor.
*  User needs to objectively compare multiple design options (Task 7.6).
*  User needs to determine the ROI, NPV, IRR, or Payback Period of a proposed change.
*  **Primary Tasks:** 6.4 (Define Change Strategy), 7.6 (Analyze Potential Value and Recommend Solution).

#### Do not use when
*  The initiative is purely mandated by strict regulatory compliance where financial return is irrelevant (though cost minimization might still apply).
*  No financial or quantifiable data is available yet.
