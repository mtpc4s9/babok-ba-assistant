### Scope Modelling (BABOK 10.41)

#### Overview
Scope models define the boundaries of a business domain or solution. They provide the basis for understanding what is being analyzed, what will be delivered, and what is explicitly excluded. Scope models are typically represented as a combination of diagrams (like Context Diagrams or Use Case Models), matrices, and textual explanations.

#### When to Use
*  User needs to define contractual obligations or estimate project effort.
*  User needs to justify in-scope/out-of-scope decisions in requirements analysis.
*  User needs to clarify the span of control, relevance of elements, and where effort will be applied.
*  **Primary Tasks:** 3.1 (Plan Business Analysis Approach), 6.1 (Analyze Current State), 6.4 (Define Change Strategy), 7.1 (Specify and Model Requirements), 7.4 (Define Requirements Architecture).

#### Do not use when
*  The boundaries are highly complex, completely dependent on the emergent position of stakeholders (horizons), and cannot be defined by traditional limits.
*  The objective is to detail the sequential flow of work (Use **10.35 Process Modelling** instead).

*"To accurately define the boundaries and prevent scope creep, I need to understand the exact limits of our analysis. Please clarify:*
1. *What **Type of Scope** are we modelling? (Scope of Control, Scope of Need, Scope of Solution, or Scope of Change)?*
2. *What are the major elements explicitly **In-Scope**? (e.g., specific processes, departments, or IT features).*
3. *What are the major elements explicitly **Out-of-Scope**? (e.g., things stakeholders might assume are included, but are definitely excluded).*
4. *Are there any critical **Assumptions** or **External Interfaces** (elements that sit on the boundary and interact with the solution)?"*

Do NOT proceed to generate the scope specification below until the user provides these boundary parameters.
