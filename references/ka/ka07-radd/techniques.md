## Task 7.1: Specify and Model Requirements

*   **Primary:**
    *   **Use Cases and Scenarios:** Used to model the desired behavior of a solution by showing interactions between an actor and the solution to achieve a specific goal.
    *   **User Stories:** Used to specify requirements as a brief, concise statement about what a specific stakeholder needs to do to deliver value (Who, What, Why).
    *   **Prototyping:** Used to assist stakeholders in visualizing the appearance and capabilities of a planned solution (uncovering missing functional requirements).
    *   **Process Modelling:** Used to show the sequential flow of work, steps, or activities that must be performed.
    *   **Data Modelling:** Used to visually represent the elements important to the business and the significant relationships among them (e.g., Class Diagrams, ERDs).
*   **Common Secondary:**
    *   **Business Rules Analysis:** Used to express and model the rules that shape day-to-day business behavior independently of how they are enforced.
    *   **State Modelling:** Used to specify the different possible states of an entity and the events that trigger state transitions.
*   **Other Secondary:** Acceptance and Evaluation Criteria, Business Capability Analysis, Business Model Canvas, Concept Modelling, Data Dictionary, Data Flow Diagrams, Decision Modelling, Functional Decomposition, Glossary, Interface Analysis, Non-Functional Requirements Analysis, Organizational Modelling, Roles and Permissions Matrix, Root Cause Analysis, Scope Modelling, Sequence Diagrams, Stakeholder List/Map/Personas.

## Task 7.2: Verify Requirements

*   **Primary:**
    *   **Reviews:** Used to inspect requirements documentation (formally or informally) to identify defects and ensure acceptable quality (Atomic, Unambiguous, Testable).
    *   **Acceptance and Evaluation Criteria:** Used to ensure that requirements are stated clearly enough to devise a set of tests that prove they have been met.
*   **Other Secondary:** Item Tracking, Metrics and KPIs.

## Task 7.3: Validate Requirements

*   **Primary:**
    *   **Metrics and Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):** Used to select appropriate performance measures to ensure the requirement delivers the expected business value.
    *   **Financial Analysis:** Used to define the financial benefits associated with requirements to prove they are worth the investment.
*   **Common Secondary:**
    *   **Reviews:** Used to confirm whether or not the stakeholders agree that their needs and business objectives are met.
*   **Other Secondary:** Acceptance and Evaluation Criteria, Document Analysis, Item Tracking, Risk Analysis and Management.

## Task 7.4: Define Requirements Architecture

*   **Primary:**
    *   **Scope Modelling:** Used to identify the elements and boundaries of the requirements architecture.
    *   **Functional Decomposition:** Used to break down an organizational unit or product scope into component parts to understand interrelationships.
*   **Common Secondary:**
    *   **Data Modelling:** Used to describe the requirements structure as it relates to data consistency.
*   **Other Secondary:** Interviews, Organizational Modelling, Workshops.

## Task 7.5: Define Design Options

*   **Primary:**
    *   **Benchmarking and Market Analysis:** Used to identify and analyze existing solutions (e.g., Commercial Off-The-Shelf products) when considering "Purchase" approaches.
    *   **Vendor Assessment:** Used to couple the assessment of a third-party solution with an assessment of the vendor's viability.
*   **Common Secondary:**
    *   **Brainstorming / Workshops:** Used collaboratively to identify improvement opportunities and generate creative design options.
*   **Other Secondary:** Document Analysis, Interviews, Lessons Learned, Mind Mapping, Root Cause Analysis, Survey or Questionnaire.

## Task 7.6: Analyze Potential Value and Recommend Solution

*   **Primary:**
    *   **Business Cases:** Used to assess recommendations against business goals and objectives, comparing expected costs vs. expected benefits.
    *   **Decision Analysis:** Used to support the objective assessment and ranking of different design options.
    *   **Financial Analysis:** Used to evaluate the financial return (ROI, NPV, TCO) of different options and choose the best possible return on investment.
*   **Common Secondary:**
    *   **Risk Analysis and Management:** Used to identify and manage the risks that could negatively affect the potential value of the recommended solution.
*   **Other Secondary:** Acceptance and Evaluation Criteria, Backlog Management, Brainstorming, Business Model Canvas, Estimation, Focus Groups, Interviews, Metrics and KPIs, Survey or Questionnaire, SWOT Analysis, Workshops.
