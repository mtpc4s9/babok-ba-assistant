## Task 4.1: Prepare for Elicitation
*   **Primary:**
    *   **Stakeholder List, Map, or Personas:** Used to determine who should be consulted while preparing for the elicitation, who should participate, and the appropriate roles.
    *   **Estimation:** Used to estimate the time and effort required for the elicitation and the associated cost.
*   **Common Secondary:**
    *   **Brainstorming / Mind Mapping:** Used to collaboratively identify and reach consensus about which sources of info should be consulted and which elicitation techniques might be most effective.
    *   **Document Analysis:** Used to identify and assess candidate sources of supporting materials.
*   **Other Secondary:** Data Mining, Interviews, Risk Analysis and Management.

## Task 4.2: Conduct Elicitation
*   **Primary:**
    *   **Interviews:** Used to ask questions of stakeholders to uncover needs, identify problems, or discover opportunities.
    *   **Workshops:** Used to elicit business analysis information from a group of people in a collaborative, facilitated way.
    *   **Observation:** Used to gain insight about how work is currently done, possibly in different locations and in different circumstances.
    *   **Document Analysis:** Used to review existing systems, contracts, business procedures and policies, standards, and regulations.
*   **Common Secondary:**
    *   **Survey or Questionnaire:** Used to elicit business analysis information from a group of people in a structured way and in a relatively short period of time.
    *   **Prototyping:** Used to elicit and validate stakeholders' needs through an iterative process that creates a model of requirements or designs.
*   **Other Secondary:** Benchmarking, Brainstorming, Business Rules Analysis, Collaborative Games, Concept Modelling, Data Mining, Data Modelling, Focus Groups, Interface Analysis, Mind Mapping, Process Analysis, Process Modelling.

## Task 4.3: Confirm Elicitation Results
*   **Primary:**
    *   **Reviews:** Used to confirm a set of elicitation results. Could be informal or formal depending on the risks.
    *   **Document Analysis:** Used to confirm elicitation results against source information or other existing documents.
*   **Common Secondary:**
    *   **Interviews:** Used to confirm the business analysis information and to confirm that the integration of that information is correct.
    *   **Workshops:** Used to conduct reviews of the drafted elicitation results using any level of formality.

## Task 4.4: Communicate Business Analysis Information
*   **Primary:**
    *   **Reviews:** Used to provide stakeholders with an opportunity to express feedback, request required adjustments, and agree or provide approvals.
*   **Common Secondary:**
    *   **Workshops:** Used to provide stakeholders with an opportunity to express feedback and to understand required adjustments, responses, and actions.
    *   **Interviews:** Used to individually communicate information to stakeholders.

## Task 4.5: Manage Stakeholder Collaboration
*   **Primary:**
    *   **Stakeholder List, Map, or Personas:** Used to determine who is available to participate, show the informal relationships between stakeholders, and understand who should be consulted.
    *   **Collaborative Games:** Used to stimulate teamwork and collaboration by temporarily immersing participants in a safe and fun situation to share knowledge.
*   **Common Secondary:**
    *   **Lessons Learned:** Used to understand stakeholders' satisfaction or dissatisfaction, and offer them an opportunity to help improve the working relationships.
    *   **Risk Analysis and Management:** Used to identify and manage risks as they relate to stakeholder involvement, participation, and engagement.
