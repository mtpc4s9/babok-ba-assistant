## Top 6 Elicitation & Collaboration Traps

### 1. The "Elicitation Phase" Myth (KA04 Overview)
*   **The Trap:** Treating Elicitation as a finite "phase" that ends once the BRD is signed off.
*   **BABOK Standard:** Elicitation and collaboration is *continuous* and ongoing as long as business analysis work is occurring. It is never a single phase.

### 2. Confirmation Bias & Leading the Witness (Task 4.2)
*   **The Trap:** Asking questions designed to validate the BA's preconceived solution (e.g., "Don't you think a dropdown menu here is the best way to fix this?").
*   **BABOK Standard:** The BA must remain objective. There is a high risk of unintentionally leading the interviewee. Elicitation is about drawing out *actual* needs, not validating early assumptions.

### 3. Confirmed vs. Approved (Task 4.3 vs Task 5.5)
*   **The Trap:** Assuming that because a stakeholder "Confirmed" the elicitation results, the requirement is "Approved" for construction.
*   **BABOK Standard:** *Confirming* (4.3) only means "Yes, your notes accurately reflect what I said." *Approving* (5.5) means "Yes, I agree to spend organizational resources to build this." They are entirely different tasks with different authority levels.

### 4. The "One-Size-Fits-All" Communication (Task 4.4)
*   **The Trap:** Sending a 200-page System Requirements Specification (SRS) to the Executive Sponsor and expecting them to read and sign it.
*   **BABOK Standard:** Business analysis information must be tailored and packaged in a format, tone, and style appropriate for the specific target audience (e.g., Executive Summary for Sponsors).

### 5. Collaboration vs. Communication (Task 4.5 vs Task 4.4)
*   **The Trap:** Believing that sending weekly status emails means you are effectively managing stakeholder collaboration.
*   **BABOK Standard:** Communication is a push of information. *Collaboration* (4.5) requires active, bi-directional engagement to secure commitments, resolve conflicts, and maintain trust.

### 6. The "Unplanned is Invalid" Trap (Task 4.2)
*   **The Trap:** Ignoring critical business rules gathered during a casual hallway chat because it wasn't a formal, scheduled workshop.
*   **BABOK Standard:** Elicitation can be both *planned* (workshops) and *unplanned* (ad-hoc conversations). Unplanned elicitation is perfectly valid and often triggers deeper planned activities.
