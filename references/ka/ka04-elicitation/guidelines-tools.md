# KA04: Elicitation and Collaboration - Guidelines & Tools

> **🤖 AI AGENT INSTRUCTION (PRE-FLIGHT CHECKLIST):**
> You MUST execute this checklist before generating any artifacts, questionnaires, or plans for KA04. 
> *   **Guidelines** provide instructions or descriptions on *why* or *how* to undertake a task.
> *   **Tools** are objects or data *used* to perform the task. 
> 
> **⚠️ INTERACTIVE SHIELD (ANTI-HALLUCINATION):**
> If a critical guideline/tool is missing, or if there is no real-world evidence of stakeholder preferences, **HALT execution**, flag the **Missing Risk**, and prompt the User for an Elicitation Interview. Do not make up mock constraints.

---

## Task 4.1: Prepare for Elicitation
*   **Guidelines (How/Why):**
    *   **Business Analysis Approach** (Output 3.1): Sets the general strategy, methodology, and expected level of detail for elicitation.
    *   **Business Objectives** (Output 6.2): Describes the desired direction to shape the elicitation events.
*   **Tools (What to Use):**
    *   **Potential Value** (Output 6.2): Used to shape elicitation events based on value to be realized.
    *   **Existing Business Analysis Information** (External/Various): Aids in preparing for the activity.
*   **⚠️ Missing Guideline Risk (CRITICAL):** Without **Business Objectives**, the scope of the elicitation activity will lack strategic boundaries, leading to Scope Creep and wasting stakeholder time on irrelevant topics.

## Task 4.2: Conduct Elicitation
*   **Guidelines (How/Why):**
    *   **Business Analysis Approach** (Output 3.1): Influences how the activity is performed.
    *   **Stakeholder Engagement Approach** (Output 3.2): Dictates the collaboration and communication styles effective for these specific stakeholders.
*   **Tools (What to Use):**
    *   **Supporting Materials** (Output 4.1 / External): Information, tools, or equipment used during the session.
    *   **Existing Business Analysis Information** (External/Various): Guides the questions posed.
*   **⚠️ Missing Guideline Risk:** Without the **Stakeholder Engagement Approach**, the AI Agent or BA might apply an aggressive workshop technique to a stakeholder who prefers private, asynchronous document analysis, destroying rapport.

## Task 4.3: Confirm Elicitation Results
*   **Guidelines (How/Why):**
    *   **Elicitation Activity Plan** (Output 4.1): Used to guide which alternative sources and results are to be compared.
*   **Tools (What to Use):**
    *   **Existing Business Analysis Information** (External/Various): Used to confirm results or develop additional follow-up questions.
*   **⚠️ Missing Guideline Risk:** Without the **Elicitation Activity Plan**, there is no baseline to compare the raw results against, making it impossible to determine if the activity met its original goals.

## Task 4.4: Communicate Business Analysis Information
*   **Guidelines (How/Why):**
    *   **Business Analysis Approach** (Output 3.1): Describes *how* (level of detail/formality) information will be disseminated.
    *   **Information Management Approach** (Output 3.4): Helps determine how information will be packaged and communicated.
*   **Tools (What to Use):**
    *   *(Relies primarily on Inputs: Business Analysis Information to be communicated).*
*   **⚠️ Missing Guideline Risk:** If the **Information Management Approach** is missing, the AI Agent will not know the correct format for the package, falling into the "One Size Fits All" trap (e.g., sending a raw backlog to a C-Level sponsor).

## Task 4.5: Manage Stakeholder Collaboration
*   **Guidelines (How/Why):**
    *   **Business Analysis Approach** (Output 3.1): Describes the level of collaboration required from each group.
*   **Tools (What to Use):**
    *   **Business Objectives** & **Future State Description** (Output 6.2): Used to focus diverse stakeholders on a common vision.
    *   **Risk Analysis Results** (Output 6.3): Highlights stakeholder-related risks that must be addressed.
*   **⚠️ Missing Guideline Risk:** Without **Business Objectives** or the **Future State Description**, there is no shared goal or "North Star". Stakeholders will easily disengage or fall into conflict due to misaligned personal agendas.
