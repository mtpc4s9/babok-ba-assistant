## Task 6.1: Analyze Current State
*   **Primary:**
    *   **SWOT Analysis:** Used to evaluate the strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats to the current state enterprise.
    *   **Root Cause Analysis:** Provides an understanding of the underlying causes of any problems in the current state in order to further clarify a business need.
    *   **Business Capability Analysis:** Identifies gaps and prioritizes them in relation to value and risk.
    *   **Document Analysis:** Analyzes existing documentation about the current state, including competitor information, supplier agreements, and performance metrics.
*   **Common Secondary:**
    *   **Observation:** Used to gain insight about how work is currently done (uncovering implicit needs).
    *   **Process Analysis:** Identifies opportunities to improve the current state.
*   **Other Secondary:** Benchmarking and Market Analysis, Business Model Canvas, Business Cases, Concept Modelling, Data Mining, Financial Analysis, Focus Groups, Functional Decomposition, Interviews, Item Tracking, Lessons Learned, Metrics and KPIs, Mind Mapping, Organizational Modelling, Process Modelling, Risk Analysis and Management, Scope Modelling, Survey or Questionnaire, Vendor Assessment, Workshops.

## Task 6.2: Define Future State
*   **Primary:**
    *   **Business Model Canvas:** Used to plan strategy for the enterprise by mapping out the needed infrastructure, target customer base, financial cost structure, and revenue streams required to fulfill the value proposition.
    *   **Balanced Scorecard:** Used to set targets for measuring the future state.
    *   **Decision Analysis:** Used to compare the different future state options and understand which is the best choice.
*   **Common Secondary:**
    *   **Metrics and Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):** Used to determine when the organization has succeeded in achieving the business objectives.
    *   **Benchmarking and Market Analysis:** Used to make decisions about future state business objectives by comparing against industry leaders.
*   **Other Secondary:** Acceptance and Evaluation Criteria, Brainstorming, Business Capability Analysis, Business Cases, Decision Modelling, Financial Analysis, Functional Decomposition, Interviews, Lessons Learned, Mind Mapping, Organizational Modelling, Process Modelling, Prototyping, Scope Modelling, Survey or Questionnaire, SWOT Analysis, Vendor Assessment, Workshops.

## Task 6.3: Assess Risks
*   **Primary:**
    *   **Risk Analysis and Management:** Used to identify and manage risks to the initiative or the enterprise.
    *   **Root Cause Analysis:** Used to identify and address the underlying problem creating a risk.
    *   **Financial Analysis:** Used to understand the potential effect of risks on the financial value of the solution.
*   **Common Secondary:**
    *   **Brainstorming:** Used to collaboratively identify potential risks for assessment.
    *   **Interviews / Workshops:** Used to understand what stakeholders think might be risks and the various factors of those risks.
*   **Other Secondary:** Business Cases, Decision Analysis, Document Analysis, Lessons Learned, Mind Mapping, Survey or Questionnaire.

## Task 6.4: Define Change Strategy
*   **Primary:**
    *   **Business Cases:** Used to capture information about the recommended change strategy and other potential strategies that were assessed but not recommended.
    *   **Business Capability Analysis:** Used to prioritize capability gaps (gap analysis) in relation to value and risk to determine the necessary transition states.
    *   **Benchmarking and Market Analysis:** Used to make decisions about which change strategy is appropriate.
    *   **Estimation:** Used to determine timelines for activities within the change strategy.
*   **Common Secondary:**
    *   **Decision Analysis:** Used to compare different change strategies and choose which is most appropriate.
    *   **Financial Analysis:** Used to understand the potential value associated with a change strategy, and evaluate strategies against targets set for return on investments.
*   **Other Secondary:** Balanced Scorecard, Brainstorming, Business Model Canvas, Focus Groups, Functional Decomposition, Interviews, Lessons Learned, Mind Mapping, Organizational Modelling, Process Modelling, Scope Modelling, SWOT Analysis, Vendor Assessment, Workshops.
